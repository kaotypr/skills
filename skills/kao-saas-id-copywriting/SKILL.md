# Indonesian SaaS Copywriting

Write SaaS product copy in Bahasa Indonesia that converts Gen-Z (born 1997–2012) and Millennials (born 1981–1996). Indonesian SaaS copy operates in a "semi-formal conversational" register — warm enough to feel human, credible enough to build trust — with strategic English code-switching for technical terms and Bahasa for emotional resonance.

Three principles govern everything:
1. **Community precedes individual** — headlines, testimonials, and CTAs signal that joining this product means joining a community
2. **Remove friction before adding benefits** — the "Tanpa" (without) pattern outperforms feature-stacking because Indonesian consumers evaluate what they *won't lose* before what they'll gain
3. **Authenticity = register accuracy** — using "gue" for Gen-Z investors and "Anda" for HR managers both feel authentic because they match the audience's actual language. Wrong register breaks trust instantly.

---

## Step 1: Determine Context

Before writing any copy, establish these four dimensions:

| Dimension | Options | Impact |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| **Product type** | Consumer app, SME tool, Enterprise SaaS, Fintech, Developer tool | Drives register + pronoun |
| **Target audience** | Gen-Z consumer, Millennial professional, SME owner, Enterprise buyer | Drives vocabulary + code-switching level |
| **Platform** | Landing page, In-app UX, TikTok, Instagram, LinkedIn, Email, Pricing page | Drives register + format |
| **Objective** | Awareness, Conversion, Onboarding, Retention, Upsell | Drives persuasion pattern |

If the user hasn't specified these, ask before writing. Getting context wrong wastes everyone's time.

## Step 2: Select Register & Pronouns

### Register decision tree

```
Is the product enterprise/B2B SaaS (HRIS, ERP, accounting)?
  → YES: Use "Anda" register (formal-professional)
  → NO: Is this social media for urban Gen-Z?
    → YES: Use "Gue/Lo" register (casual/gaul) — TikTok, Reels, push notifications only
    → NO: Use "Kamu" register (semi-formal conversational) — this is the default
```

### Register details

**"Kamu" (semi-formal conversational)** — the default for consumer/SME SaaS
- Pronouns: kamu, -mu (possessive suffix like "bisnismu")
- Particles: "ya" (right?), "yuk" (let's), "nih" (here/this), "dong" (please/c'mon) — these are mandatory, not decorative. They're what separate human copy from robotic translated copy
- Tone: knowledgeable friend, not corporation issuing commands
- Use for: landing pages, onboarding, help docs, email, in-app UX
- Examples: Bibit, Majoo, Stockbit

**"Anda" (formal-professional)** — enterprise/B2B only
- Pronouns: Anda, Saya
- No slang particles. Professional but not stiff.
- Use for: enterprise landing pages, B2B sales pages, case studies
- Examples: Mekari, Xendit, Jurnal

**"Gue/Lo" (casual/gaul)** — urban Gen-Z social media only
- Pronouns: gue (I), lo (you), abbreviations like "bgt" (banget), "gak" (tidak)
- Jakarta slang, very informal
- Use for: TikTok, Instagram Reels, push notifications targeting urban youth
- Never use on landing pages or official product pages

**Critical rule**: Never mix pronouns within a single page or flow. If a landing page uses "kamu," every section uses "kamu."

## Step 3: Select Persuasion Pattern

Use Indonesia-native patterns as the primary frameworks. Western frameworks are adapted fallbacks, not defaults.

### Primary patterns (prefer these)

**"Tanpa" (Without) Pattern** — the most powerful structure for Indonesian SaaS copy. Lead with friction removal, not benefit addition.
- Template: "Tanpa [cost X]. Tanpa [requirement Y]. Tanpa [friction Z]."
- Use when: removing objections, pricing pages, sign-up flows, free tier messaging

**"Dipercaya Oleh" (Trusted By) Pattern** — the primary trust architecture.
- Template: "Dipercaya oleh [number]+ [bisnis/pengguna/merchant] di [number]+ kota di Indonesia"
- Always include specific numbers — vague claims don't convert
- Use when: building credibility, landing page social proof sections, above-the-fold trust signals

**"Gotong Royong" (Communal) Frame** — positions product as enabling collective progress.
- Keywords: "Bersama" (together), "Komunitas" (community), "Kebersamaan" (togetherness)
- Reframe individual tools as community enablers
- Use when: community-driven products, investment platforms, collaborative tools

**Soft CTA with Particles** — Indonesian audiences prefer invitation over command.
- "Yuk, coba sekarang!" beats "Coba sekarang"
- "Mulai gratis" beats bare "Daftar"
- Always include WhatsApp as a conversion channel for SME-facing products

### Adapted Western frameworks (use as fallbacks)

**AIDA** — Attention phase must use social proof/community hooks, not individual benefit. "Dipercaya oleh 35.000+ bisnis di Indonesia" captures attention better than "Save 10 hours per week."

**PAS** — Agitate step must be culturally dampened. Use colloquial discomfort words — "pusing" (headache), "ribet" (complicated), "repot" (troublesome) — not aggressive amplification. "Kelola SDM Tidak Perlu Pusing Lagi!" works. "You're bleeding money every day!" does not.

**StoryBrand** — Community transformation over individual hero journey. The hero isn't one business owner — it's the entire UMKM community rising together.

For detailed examples, templates, and 5+ real-world instances of each pattern, read `references/persuasion-patterns.md`.

## Step 4: Write Copy

### English code-switching rules

**Retain English for** (never translate these): dashboard, analytics, login, subscribe, download, upload, setting, template, feedback, update, account, password, notification, upgrade, API, cloud, CRM, POS, SaaS, AI, UX, onboarding

**Use Bahasa for**: all emotional language, benefit statements, CTAs, headlines, value propositions

**The pattern**: English nouns inside Bahasa sentence structures.
- Correct: "Cek dashboard kamu" — English noun, Bahasa frame
- Wrong: "Check your dashboard" — full English in Bahasa-primary copy
- Wrong: "Cek papan instrumen kamu" — forced translation sounds robotic

**Jaksel-style mixing** (English fillers like "which is", "literally", "basically") — only for urban Gen-Z social media content. Never on landing pages or mass-market copy. Outside Jakarta it reads as pretentious.

**Text expansion**: Bahasa runs 30-40% longer than English. "VIEW ALL" (7 chars) → "LIHAT SEMUA" (10 chars). Account for this in all UI copy — shorter is better.

### Landing page headline patterns

Use these templates, then follow with a barrier-removal subheadline:

| Pattern | Template | When to use |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| Pain-removal | "Kelola [domain] Tidak Perlu [colloquial pain] Lagi!" | When the product solves a known frustration |
| Rotating persona | "[Verb] untuk [Persona A / Persona B / Persona C / Semua]" | When the product serves multiple segments |
| Community | "[Activity] Bersama [Komunitas / Teman / Keluarga]" | Community-driven products |
| Aspiration + simplicity | "Bikin [asset] kamu [verb lebih keras/cepat/pintar] demi [aspirational goal]" | Consumer finance, productivity |
| All-in-one solution | "Aplikasi [domain] terlengkap untuk [verb] [bisnismu/timmu] jadi lebih [adjective]" | Multi-feature platforms |

**Subheadline always removes barriers**: "Daftar 100% online", "Gratis, tanpa biaya X", "Buat akun dalam X menit"

### Trust signal hierarchy (use this priority order)

1. Government/regulatory certification — OJK, Bank Indonesia, Kominfo, ISO 27001
2. Community scale numbers — "Dipercaya oleh X+ bisnis"
3. KOL/celebrity endorsement — micro-influencers (25-80% engagement) outperform mega-influencers (2-5%)
4. Named testimonials with before/after metrics — "Sebelumnya 5 hari. Sekarang 1 hari."
5. BUMN/enterprise client logos — Antam, Pelindo, Biofarma etc.
6. Local payment method logos — GoPay, OVO, DANA, QRIS (digital wallets = 39% of transactions)
7. Syariah/Islamic compliance — DSN-MUI certification (mainstream, not niche)

### Pricing copy rules

- Never show monthly price alone. Always micro-anchor: "Rp90.000/bulan" → "Hanya Rp3.000/hari"
- Lead with "Gratis" — free tier/trial is table stakes in Indonesia
- Use "Tanpa" for hidden-cost fears: "Tanpa biaya tersembunyi. Tanpa minimum deposit. Tanpa kontrak."
- Enterprise: "Harga disesuaikan dengan kebutuhan bisnis Anda"
- Display local payment logos prominently
- Time promotions to payday cycles (tanggal 25-31)
- Number formatting: decimals use commas (5,25), thousands use periods (Rp1.526,75), dates DD/MM/YYYY

### Platform-specific conventions

**In-app UX**: Localize, don't translate. "Lagi cari apa nih?" not "Mencari sesuatu?". Particles create personality. Error messages empathize then direct — never blame the user: "Pembayaran gagal. Mohon cek metode pembayarannya."

**TikTok**: Casual/gaul register. Hook in 1-3 seconds. Authentic > polished. Peak posting: 07:00-09:00, 12:00-14:00, 19:00-23:00 WIB.

**Instagram**: Semi-formal to casual. Educational carousels, lifestyle content. Reels for reach, Stories for engagement.

**LinkedIn Indonesia**: Semi-formal to formal. Bahasa with English professional vocabulary. Case studies and thought leadership.

**Email**: Subject lines with "Gratis", "Eksklusif", "Terbatas", "Hemat". Personalize: "Hai [Nama], kamu dapat voucher diskon..." Time to payday cycles. Note: Indonesian email open rates are low (~21.67%) — WhatsApp is the dominant SME channel.

## Step 5: Self-Check Against Anti-Patterns

Before presenting copy, verify none of these are present:

| Anti-pattern | What it looks like | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Paragraph headline | 20+ word headline with zero scan value | Cut to under 10 words, lead with verb or benefit |
| "Terlengkap/Terbaik" inflation | Unsupported superlatives | Add proof or remove the superlative |
| English-first for mass market | Full English copy targeting warung owners | Rewrite in Bahasa with English only for tech nouns |
| Feature-spec without benefit | "Biometric face recognition with geo-locator" | → "Cukup selfie, bebas terkontaminasi!" |
| Stiff translation | "Mencari sesuatu?" | → "Lagi cari apa nih?" |
| Wrong register | "Anda" for Gen-Z investment app | Switch to "kamu" |
| Pronoun mixing | "Kamu" in headline, "Anda" in body | Unify throughout the page |
| Aggressive agitation | "You're losing millions every day!" | → "Tidak perlu repot lagi" (soft pain acknowledgment) |

Provide 2-3 variations with different approaches, labeled clearly:
