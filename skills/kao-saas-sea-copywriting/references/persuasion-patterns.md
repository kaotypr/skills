## JTBD (Jobs to be Done)

The most culturally portable framework for SEA SaaS. It focuses on functional outcomes rather than culturally specific emotional triggers, avoids idiom-heavy narrative, and works across proficiency levels because it mirrors how people naturally describe their problems.

### Structure
"When I'm [situation], help me [job], so I can [desired outcome]"

The power of JTBD in SEA is that it sidesteps cultural landmines entirely. No idioms to misunderstand, no emotional triggers that vary by country — just a clear articulation of what the user needs.

### Real examples

**Aspire:** "Financial OS for Modern Businesses"
- Job: managing global business finances
- Outcome: scale without financial complexity

**Wise:** "The fast way to send money abroad"
- Job: international money transfer
- Outcome: speed and savings (demonstrated with real-time calculators)

**Carousell:** "Every kinda thing, for every kinda person"
- Job: buying and selling secondhand
- Outcome: universal accessibility

**SleekFlow:** "Omnichannel messaging platform for AI conversions"
- Job: unifying customer conversations
- Outcome: more conversions through AI

**StoreHub:** "Run Your Business, Not Your POS"
- Job: business management
- Outcome: freedom from point-of-sale headaches

### Templates

**Hero headline:**
```
The [adjective] way to [job].
[Job verb] your [domain] — without the [pain].
[Product]. [Job outcome] made simple.
```

**Feature section:**
```
When your team needs to [job], [Product] [how it helps].
Stop [painful current process]. Start [desired outcome].
```

**Onboarding:**
```
Tell us about your [business/team/workflow] so we can help you [job] faster.
What's the first thing you want to [job verb]?
```

### Usage tips
- Keep the "situation" grounded in daily work reality, not abstract business goals
- The "outcome" should be concrete and measurable when possible
- Avoid aspirational language that sounds different across cultures — "be your best self" means different things in Singapore vs. Thailand

## Social Proof Amplification

In collectivist cultures, social proof has amplified persuasive power compared to individualist Western markets. Robert Cialdini's research confirms: "In more collectivist cultures, if a lot of your peers are doing something, that's a more powerful impetus for you than for people in more individualistic cultures."

### Structure
Always include specific numbers and local references. Vague claims don't convert.

### Real examples

**Xendit:** Lists specific local payment rails (QRIS, GCash, PromptPay, VietQR, FPX) — evidence of deep local integration, not just a claim.

**Aspire:** Names regional customers: Endowus, Zenyum, Tech In Asia, AirAsia, Love Bonito — the signal is "people like you, in your market, already trust this."

**SleekFlow:** G2 badges (4.6, 200+ reviews) + Meta Select Tier Business Partner status as exclusive trust signaling.

**Grab:** Government awards and hard economic impact numbers (S$5.2 billion contribution to Singapore's economy).

**Shopee:** Scale as social proof: "Cheaper, Faster On Shopee" — two words capturing both value propositions.

### Templates

**Standard community proof:**
```
Trusted by [number]+ [businesses/teams/creators] across Southeast Asia.
Join [number]+ teams in [number]+ countries who [benefit verb].
```

**With local specificity:**
```
Trusted by [Local Brand A], [Local Brand B], and [number]+ more across [market].
From [startup name] to [enterprise name] — teams across SEA rely on [Product].
```

**With metric:**
```
[Number]+ [businesses/teams] use [Product] to [specific outcome] every [period].
Processing [volume]+ [transactions/messages/orders] across Southeast Asia.
```

**With growth signal:**
```
[Number]+ new teams join every [period]. See why.
The fastest-growing [category] platform in Southeast Asia.
```

**Group decision facilitation:**
```
Share with your team →
See why [number]+ teams chose [Product] →
Forward this to your team — they'll thank you.
```

### Usage tips
- Local brand logos carry more weight than international ones — always prioritize regional companies
- Wisdom-of-the-crowd signals ("Join 50,000+ teams") outperform individual expert endorsements
- Add "Share with your team" CTAs — reflects how SEA purchasing decisions involve consensus
- Update numbers regularly — stale numbers suggest stagnant growth
- Nano-influencer quotes (2K-10K followers) achieve 4x higher engagement than celebrity endorsements

## Before-After-Bridge

Performs exceptionally well in SEA when kept simple. The Before state uses plain, relatable language; the After state emphasizes concrete outcomes; the Bridge is the product, kept short.

### Real examples

**StoreHub** runs their entire website on this framework:
- "From chaos to control" (F&B)
- "From clutter to clarity" (Retail)
- "From friction to flow" (Services)

**Funding Societies / Modalku:**
- Before: "Traditional loan applications take weeks"
- After: "Lightning-fast loans up to S$150K, disbursed in 24 hours"
- Bridge: Funding Societies

**Wise:**
- Before: "Banks inflate the mid-market exchange rate to hide fees"
- After: "Send money at the real exchange rate"
- Bridge: Wise (with real-time calculator showing exact savings)

### Templates

**Hero section:**
```
From [pain] to [gain].
[Product]: the bridge from [current state] to [desired state].
```

**Feature comparison:**
```
Before [Product]: [specific pain, quantified if possible].
After [Product]: [specific outcome, quantified].
```

**Testimonial-driven:**
```
"We went from [old metric] to [new metric] in [timeframe]."
— [Name], [Role] at [Local Company]
```

**Vertical-specific:**
```
[Industry] teams: go from [industry pain] to [industry outcome].
```

### Usage tips
- The "From X to Y" structure is inherently scannable and works across proficiency levels
- Keep both states to 3-5 words for maximum impact
- Use alliteration when natural (chaos→control, clutter→clarity) but don't force it
- Pair with a specific metric when available: "From 5-day payroll to 1-day payroll"

## Friction Removal

The English equivalent of the Indonesian "Tanpa" (Without) pattern. SEA consumers evaluate what they won't lose before what they'll gain — leading with barrier removal outperforms benefit-stacking.

### Structure
Stack 3-5 friction removals in rhythmic succession. Each removal should address a real, specific objection.

### Real examples

**Aspire:** "Get Started. It's Free."
— Clean, low-friction. Three words that remove cost, commitment, and complexity barriers simultaneously.

**Xendit:** "Try Xendit for free in just 5 minutes!"
— Removes cost barrier + time barrier in one sentence.

**Carousell:** "LIST IN 30 SECONDS."
— Removes complexity barrier with speed promise.

**Wise:** Real-time savings calculator showing exact amounts.
— Removes the "how much will I actually save?" uncertainty.

### Templates

**Sign-up/trial:**
```
No credit card required. No setup fee. No long-term contract.
Try free for [X] days. Cancel anytime. Keep everything you've built.
Start in [X] minutes — no technical skills needed.
```

**Pricing page:**
```
No hidden fees. No surprise charges. What you see is what you pay.
Pay as you go. Scale up or down anytime.
Free forever for small teams. Upgrade when you're ready.
```

**Switching/migration:**
```
Switch from [competitor] in [X] minutes. No data lost. No downtime.
Import your existing [data type] — we handle the rest.
```

**Onboarding:**
```
No training needed. If you can use [simple app], you can use [Product].
Set up your workspace in 3 steps. We'll guide you through each one.
```

### Usage tips
- Each removal should address a real objection, not filler
- End the sequence with the most emotionally resonant removal
- "No credit card required" is near-mandatory for SEA — credit card penetration is low
- Pair friction removal with speed promises for maximum effect

## Adapted AIDA

The classic AIDA framework works in SEA with one critical shift: the Desire phase must emphasize collective benefit over individual aspiration.

### Phase-by-phase adaptation

| Phase | Western Default | SEA Adaptation |
|-------|----------------|----------------|
| Attention | Individual benefit hook | Social proof + community numbers |
| Interest | Feature details | Friction removal + concrete outcomes |
| Desire | ROI / individual gain | Community belonging + shared success |
| Action | Direct command CTA | Low-friction CTA with risk reduction |

### Example: Pan-SEA B2B SaaS

**Attention:** "Trusted by 12,000+ teams across Southeast Asia."
**Interest:** "No setup fee. No training needed. Your team is up and running in 15 minutes."
**Desire:** "Join the teams at [Local Brand], [Local Brand], and [Local Brand] who've cut their [process] time by 60%."
**Action:** "Try free for 14 days. No credit card required."

### Example: Singapore Fintech

**Attention:** "S$2.3 billion processed for Southeast Asian businesses last year."
**Interest:** "Multi-currency accounts, corporate cards, and expense management — one platform."
**Desire:** "See why Endowus, Zenyum, and 500+ companies manage their finances with Aspire."
**Action:** "Get Started. It's Free."

## Adapted PAS

PAS works in SEA but requires a gentler Agitate phase. Direct pain amplification can feel confrontational in face-saving cultures.

### Phase-by-phase adaptation

| Phase | Western Default | SEA Adaptation |
|-------|----------------|----------------|
| Problem | Name the pain directly | Name as shared industry challenge |
| Agitate | Amplify aggressively | Acknowledge empathetically — specific, business-focused |
| Solve | Product as individual hero | Product + community as solution |

### Example: Project Management SaaS

**Problem:** "Managing projects across time zones is messy."
**Agitate:** "Tasks get lost between apps. Deadlines slip. Your team in Manila doesn't know what your team in Singapore decided yesterday."
**Solve:** "With [Product], your entire team sees the same picture — no matter where they are. Join 8,000+ SEA teams who've stopped losing work to scattered tools."

### Example: HR SaaS

**Problem:** "Payroll across Southeast Asia means juggling six different tax systems."
**Agitate:** "Manual calculations. Compliance risk. Late nights before payday — every month."
**Solve:** "[Product] automates payroll for SG, PH, MY, ID, VN, and TH. Set up once, run payroll in minutes. Trusted by 3,000+ companies."

### Cultural note
The Agitate phase should feel like empathetic acknowledgment of a shared struggle, not fear-mongering. "Many teams across Southeast Asia struggle with…" frames the problem as normal and solvable. "You're losing thousands every day!" feels confrontational and will reduce trust.

## Headline Templates

### Outcome, Not Pain
```
[Outcome], Not [Pain].
[Verb] Your [Domain] — Not Your [Tool/Process].
[Positive Action]. Not [Negative Reality].
```

### Speed + Action
```
The [Adjective] Way to [Action].
[Action] in [Timeframe] — Not [Longer Timeframe].
From [Start] to [Finish] in [Speed].
```

### Product + Benefit
```
[Product]. [Benefit in 3-5 words].
[Product]: [Outcome] Made Simple.
[Product] — Because [Relatable Reason].
```

### From Pain to Gain
```
From [Pain] to [Gain].
[Pain State] → [Gain State].
Leave [Pain] Behind. Say Hello to [Gain].
```

### Category for Outcome
```
[Category] for [Specific Outcome].
The [Category] Platform for [Audience] Teams.
[Category] Built for [Region/Market].
```

### Community-Centered
```
Join [Number]+ [Teams/Businesses] Who [Outcome Verb].
The Platform [Number]+ SEA Teams Trust for [Domain].
Built for Teams. Loved Across Southeast Asia.
```

## Pricing Copy Templates

### Free tier
```
Free forever for [scope].
$0/month for up to [limit]. Upgrade when you outgrow it.
Start free. No credit card. No time limit.
```

### Local anchoring
```
Less than [two Grab rides / a cup of coffee / a coworking day pass] per month.
Starting at [local currency amount] — less than you spend on [relatable local item].
```

### ROI framing
```
Save your team [X] hours per week — that's [Y] hours per year.
Pay [amount]. Save [larger amount]. The math is simple.
[X] minutes of setup. [Y] hours saved every month.
```

### Friction removal in pricing
```
No hidden fees. No surprise charges. No annual contracts.
Pay as you go. Scale up or down anytime.
Switch plans in two clicks. No penalties. No waiting.
```

### Payment trust signals
```
Pay with [GrabPay / GCash / FPX / PromptPay / MoMo / bank transfer].
Secure payments via [payment processor]. Your data is encrypted.
All prices shown in your local currency.
```

## Platform-Specific Hooks

### TikTok / Short-form video
```
Hook formats (first 1-3 seconds):
"Stop scrolling if your team still uses [old method]..."
"Here's why [number]+ teams in [country] switched to..."
"POV: You just saved your team 10 hours this week"
"The [tool] that [local company] doesn't want you to know about"
```

### LinkedIn
```
Opening lines (must be compelling in first 3 lines):
"We were spending [X hours] per week on [task]. Then we tried something different."
"[Number]+ teams across Southeast Asia have one thing in common: [insight]."
"I used to think [common belief]. Here's what changed."
"Our [city] team cut [metric] by [percent]. Here's the 3-step process:"
```

### Facebook (especially Philippines)
```
Story-driven formats:
"When [Name] started her [business type] in [city], she never expected..."
"Meet the [job title] who [specific achievement] using just one tool."
"[Number] [business type] owners asked us the same question. Here's what we told them:"
```

### Email subject lines (under 40 characters)
```
"Your team's [X] problem, solved"
"[Name], [number] teams can't be wrong"
"Free for your first [X] [tasks/users]"
"[Local Company] saved [metric]. You can too"
"Quick: [benefit] in [timeframe]"
```
