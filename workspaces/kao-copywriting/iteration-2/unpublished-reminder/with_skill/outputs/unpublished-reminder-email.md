# InviteJoy — 7-Day Unpublished Invitation Reminder Email

## Approach Summary

**Audience**: Global, primarily women 25-45 planning celebrations (birthdays, weddings, baby showers, gatherings).
**Platform**: Email (transactional/lifecycle reminder).
**Objective**: Re-engagement — get the user to publish their drafted invitation.
**Tone**: Warm, supportive, gently nudging. Conversational and encouraging, not pushy.
**Cultural calibration**: Global English defaults — simple vocabulary, no idioms, universal motivations (reducing stress, feeling confident, celebrating with loved ones). Inclusive and neutral.

Three variations are provided below using different strategic frameworks. All emphasize that publishing is easy (one click), acknowledge that life is busy, and make the moment feel exciting rather than guilt-inducing.

## Notes

- **Recommended A/B test**: Test Variation 1 (BAB) against Variation 3 (PPPP). Variation 1 uses stronger contrast psychology, while Variation 3 leads with excitement. Both avoid guilt-driven language.
- **Subject line testing**: The subject lines take three different angles — completeness ("Your invitation is ready"), empathy ("Still planning?"), and social anticipation ("Your guests are going to love this"). Test all three to see which drives higher open rates.
- **Platform-specific**: All variations follow email best practices — one objective (publish), one primary CTA, conversational tone, and a P.S. that handles a secondary concern without diluting the main action.
- **Global audience fit**: All copy uses simple vocabulary, active voice, short sentences, and no idioms or culture-specific references. Motivations are universal — connection with loved ones, ease, and celebration.
- **CTA refinement**: "Publish Your Invitation" or "Publish Your Invitation Now" is specific and action-driven, avoiding weak CTAs like "Click here" or "Learn more." The word "Publish" mirrors the actual UI action for consistency.
