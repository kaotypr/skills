# Abandoned Cart / Unfinished Payment Email — InviteJoy

## Approach Summary

**Audience:** Global, primarily women 25-45 planning events (weddings, birthdays, baby showers, gatherings). Non-aggressive tone that respects their decision-making process.

**Framework:** Variation 1 uses PAS (Problem-Agitation-Solution) to gently surface the unfinished task and resolve it. Variation 2 uses BAB (Before-After-Bridge) to paint the contrast between a saved draft and a live invitation. Variation 3 uses PPPP (Picture-Promise-Prove-Push) for a storytelling-driven approach.

**Tone:** Helpful, understanding, gently urgent. Warm and conversational — like a thoughtful friend reminding you, not a store chasing a sale.

**Voice:** "Supportive, not pushy" / "Warm, not salesy" / "Helpful, not desperate"

**Persuasion levers:** Loss aversion (design might not stay saved forever), reciprocity (we saved your work), social proof (subtle), soft scarcity (template availability).

## Notes

- **Recommended A/B test:** Test Variation 1 (direct, efficient) against Variation 2 (emotional, aspirational) to see which resonates more with the primary audience. Variation 1 may perform better for time-pressed users; Variation 2 for emotionally-driven planners.
- **Subject lines:** Test subject lines with and without the word "saved" — it signals reciprocity and reduces anxiety about lost work.
- **Timing:** Send the first email 1-3 hours after cart abandonment, a second reminder 24 hours later (with a slightly different angle), and a final gentle nudge at 72 hours.
- **CTA button:** The CTA includes the price ($9.99) for transparency — this reduces checkout friction by eliminating surprise costs.
- **Global audience considerations:** All three variations use simple vocabulary, active voice, short sentences, and no idioms or culture-specific references. Motivations (saving time, reducing stress, making guests happy) are universal. The tone avoids aggressive urgency that could feel pushy in high uncertainty-avoidance cultures.
- **P.S. line:** Each variation includes a P.S. that opens a support channel. This builds trust and catches users who abandoned due to technical issues rather than indecision.
