**Approach summary**: This is a welcome email for InviteJoy, a digital invitation platform. The audience is global, primarily women 25-45 who are planning celebrations and want a simple, beautiful way to invite guests. The tone is warm, celebratory, and helpful — matching email nurture best practices. I used the **AIDA** framework (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action) because this is a brand introduction moment where we need to build excitement from zero and guide the user toward their first action: browsing templates. Global English defaults apply — simple vocabulary, no idioms, active voice, universal motivations. I also incorporated **reciprocity** (emphasizing free templates) and **simplicity** power words to lower the barrier to action.

**Notes**:
- **Variation 1** is the safest all-purpose option — clean structure, warm tone, clear steps. Works well for a global audience with mixed familiarity levels.
- **Variation 2** uses the Before/After/Bridge framework to create a small emotional contrast. Best for audiences who have felt the frustration of "ugly" group messages and want something better. Slightly more emotional.
- **Variation 3** adds a benefit list for scanners and a P.S. line as a secondary nudge. The P.S. works well in email because many readers jump straight to it. This variation also lightly seeds social proof language ("Why people love InviteJoy") without fabricating specific numbers.
- All three variations use Global English: short sentences, active voice, no idioms, no culture-specific references. Safe for a worldwide audience.
- Subject lines are kept short and personal. Each preview text extends the subject line rather than repeating it.
- Recommended A/B test: Test Variation 1 subject line ("Your first invitation is waiting") against Variation 2 ("No more boring group messages") to see whether curiosity or aspiration drives higher open rates for this audience.
- CTA buttons use low-commitment, value-driven language ("Browse Templates," "Start Creating") rather than aggressive sales language — appropriate for a free-to-use product where the goal is activation, not purchase.
