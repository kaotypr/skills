# FlowBoard — 3-Email Welcome Sequence

## Approach Summary

**Product**: FlowBoard, a project management SaaS for remote teams of 5-20 people.
**Core features**: Async standup updates, visual project timelines, automated progress reports.
**Audience**: Team leads and ops managers frustrated with too many meetings and scattered communication across tools.
**Platform**: Email (welcome/onboarding nurture sequence).
**Objective**: Activate new sign-ups — move them from "just signed up" to "experiencing first value" to "converting to paid."

**Framework strategy**:
- Email 1 (Day 0): PAS — validate their pain, introduce FlowBoard as the fix
- Email 2 (Day 2): BAB — paint the before/after transformation, drive first real usage
- Email 3 (Day 5): AIDA — build desire with specifics and social proof, push to upgrade or commit

**Voice**: Warm, conversational, peer-to-peer. Like a message from a colleague who gets it — not a marketing blast. Confident but never pushy. Short paragraphs. American English.

## Notes

**Recommended variation by audience segment:**
- **Variation 1 (Empathy-First)**: Best for team leads who are emotionally exhausted by meeting culture. The warm, conversational tone builds trust and makes FlowBoard feel like relief, not another tool to learn.
- **Variation 2 (Data-Driven)**: Best for ops managers who make decisions based on numbers and efficiency. The direct tone and specific metrics (hours saved, meeting reduction) speak their language.
- **Variation 3 (Storytelling)**: Best for founders and early-stage team leads who value authenticity. The origin story and reciprocity approach (free templates) builds a relationship before asking for a commitment.

**A/B testing suggestions:**
- Test subject lines across variations — the data-driven subject lines (Variation 2) often outperform in B2B SaaS, but the curiosity-driven lines (Variation 1) tend to get higher open rates from first-time users.
- Email 3 is the highest-leverage email to test. Try swapping the loss-aversion angle (Variation 2) against the social proof angle (Variation 3) to see which drives more upgrades.
- Consider testing send timing: Day 0 / Day 2 / Day 5 vs. Day 0 / Day 3 / Day 7 to find the optimal cadence for your audience.

**Global audience note:** All three variations use simple vocabulary, active voice, and avoid idioms or culture-specific references — making them suitable for international remote teams. If targeting specific regions, consider adjusting the testimonial names and team descriptions to match the local market.
