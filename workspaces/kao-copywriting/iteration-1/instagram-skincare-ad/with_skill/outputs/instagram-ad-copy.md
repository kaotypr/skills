# Instagram Ad Copy — Glow Theory Vitamin C Serum

## Approach Summary

**Brand**: Glow Theory
**Product**: Vitamin C Serum, $38
**Platform**: Instagram (Feed Ad + Story Ad)
**Target Audience**: Women 25-40, globally. Clean beauty advocates who are skeptical of over-hyped skincare products. They research ingredients, read reviews, and distrust exaggerated promises.
**Tone**: Honest, direct, understated confidence. No hype. No miracle claims. Speaks like a knowledgeable friend, not a billboard.
**Cultural calibration**: Global English -- simple vocabulary, no idioms, universal motivations (confidence, trust, self-care). Avoids region-specific references.

**Framework choices**:
- Feed Ad: **PAS** (Problem → Agitation → Solution) — connects with the audience's frustration with overpromising skincare brands, then positions Glow Theory as the honest alternative.
- Story Ad: **SLAP** (Stop → Look → Act → Purchase) — designed for fast, swipe-through impact with a clear visual-first structure.

**Persuasion psychology applied**: Specificity (15% concentration, real ingredient details), authority (clean formulation cues), loss aversion (what their current routine is missing), and anti-social-proof (positioning against the noise, which resonates with skeptics).

## Notes

- **Recommended A/B testing**: For the feed ad, test Variation 1 (direct, benefit-rich) against Variation 3 (contrarian honesty). The skeptic audience is likely to respond well to "what it won't do" framing, but benefit-specificity may outperform on conversion. Test both.
- **Story ad recommendation**: Variation 2 ("The Anti-Ad") is the strongest scroll-stopper for this audience because it breaks the pattern of what ads typically say. Variation 3 works well for audiences deeper in the consideration stage who actively research ingredients.
- **Visual pairing**: The honest, anti-hype tone works best with clean product photography — white or neutral backgrounds, no heavy filters, real skin texture visible. Lifestyle shots should feel candid, not staged.
- **Hashtag suggestions**: #CleanBeauty #VitaminCSerum #SkincareIngredients #NoFilterSkincare #HonestBeauty #GlowTheory
- **Global audience note**: All copy uses Global English — simple vocabulary, no idioms, no culture-specific references. The tone lands well across North America, Europe, Southeast Asia, and Australasia. For East Asian markets, consider adding more social proof elements (user counts, ratings) in a localized variant.
