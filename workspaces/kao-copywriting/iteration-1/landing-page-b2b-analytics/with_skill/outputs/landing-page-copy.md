# MetricShift Landing Page Copy — Hero Section + First Two Content Blocks

## Approach Summary

**Audience:** CMOs and marketing directors at mid-market e-commerce companies ($10M-$100M revenue). These buyers are data-literate, time-poor, and frustrated by vanity metrics that don't connect to the bottom line. They've been burned by ROAS dashboards that show "winning" campaigns that actually lose money after COGS, shipping, and returns.

**Tone:** Confident, direct, data-rich. Professional without being stiff. Speaks peer-to-peer — not vendor-to-buyer. American English.

**Key insight driving the copy:** The real pain isn't "we don't have data" — it's "we have too much data and none of it tells us what's actually profitable." The copy targets the gap between revenue attribution and profit attribution.

## Notes

- **Recommended lead variation:** Variation 1 ("Profit Truth") is the strongest for direct-response landing pages. The opening headline creates an immediate tension between perceived performance and reality — a feeling every CMO at this level has experienced.

- **A/B testing suggestion:** Test Variation 1's headline against Variation 3's headline. Both target loss aversion, but Variation 1 is more subtle (implies the problem) while Variation 3 is more direct (names the problem). Mid-market CMOs tend to respond well to directness.

- **Variation 2** works best if MetricShift has strong brand credibility signals to add (logos, case study numbers, testimonials). The ACCA framework relies on building conviction, which needs proof points. Add customer logos and a specific metric (e.g., "Teams using MetricShift have identified an average of $14,000/month in wasted ad spend") to strengthen the conviction block.

- **CTA approach:** All three variations use low-friction demo CTAs rather than free trial CTAs. For a B2B analytics product targeting C-suite buyers at this company size, a guided demo converts better than self-serve — the value is hard to see without real data in the system.

- **Social proof placeholders:** Once MetricShift has customer data, add specific proof points inline: customer logos, a named testimonial from a CMO, and a concrete metric ("identified $X in wasted spend in the first 30 days"). Specificity beats generality in B2B.
