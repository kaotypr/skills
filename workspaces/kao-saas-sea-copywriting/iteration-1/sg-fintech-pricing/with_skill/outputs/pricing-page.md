## A/B Testing Recommendations

1. **Variation A vs. B hero headline:** Test "One account. Every currency in Southeast Asia." (JTBD/direct) against "From scattered accounts to one dashboard." (Before-After/emotional). The JTBD version may convert better for users arriving from search (high intent), while the Before-After version may work better for social/referral traffic (lower intent, needs pain recognition).

2. **ROI anchor placement:** Variation C places ROI numbers directly under each tier. Test this against Variation A's approach of placing the ROI anchor only on the Growth tier. Over-anchoring on every tier may create fatigue.

3. **Competitive comparison table (Variation C):** Test the page with and without the comparison table. For users already evaluating Aspire/Airwallex, the table accelerates the decision. For users unfamiliar with competitors, it may introduce alternatives they had not considered.

4. **FAQ question about competitors:** All three variations address Aspire and Airwallex in the FAQ. Test whether naming competitors directly helps (signals confidence) or hurts (introduces consideration of alternatives).

5. **Secondary CTA — "Share with your team":** Test this against "See a demo" as the secondary action. In Singapore's SME context, purchase decisions often involve multiple stakeholders, so the share CTA may drive higher downstream conversion even if direct click rates are lower.
