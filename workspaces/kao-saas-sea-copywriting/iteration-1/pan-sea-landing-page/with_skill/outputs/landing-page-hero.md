## Variation A: Adapted PAS + Social Proof Amplification

**Framework rationale:** PAS works well for B2B project management because the pain of cross-timezone coordination is real and shared across SEA teams. The agitate phase stays business-focused and empathetic, not confrontational. Social proof amplification with community numbers and named local brands creates collectivist trust signals.

**Best suited for:** Pan-SEA (SG, PH, MY) engineering and ops leaders who feel the daily pain of scattered tools and async gaps.

### Hero copy

**Headline:**
> Keep Your Distributed Team on the Same Page

**Subheadline:**
> Real-time task boards, async video updates, and automated standups — so your team across Singapore, Manila, and KL always knows what's happening. No more lost context. No more missed deadlines.

**Social proof bar:**
> Trusted by 8,000+ teams across Southeast Asia — including Ninja Van and Love Bonito.

**Primary CTA button:**
> Try CloudTeam Free

**Secondary CTA:**
> Share with your team

**Risk-reduction micro-copy (below CTA):**
> No credit card required. Set up in 5 minutes.

### A/B testing recommendation
Test the headline against a shorter version: "One Team. Three Time Zones. Zero Missed Updates." to compare outcome-focused vs. pain-removal framing.

## Variation B: JTBD + Friction Removal

**Framework rationale:** JTBD is the most culturally portable framework for SEA. It focuses on the functional job ("coordinate work across time zones") and a concrete desired outcome. Paired with friction removal, it addresses the specific anxieties mid-size teams have about adopting new tools. Keeps language at Grade 5-7 readability and avoids idioms.

**Best suited for:** Engineering managers evaluating tools for the first time who need a clear, jargon-free explanation of what CloudTeam does and why it is worth trying.

### Hero copy

**Headline:**
> Coordinate Work Across Time Zones — Without the Chaos

**Subheadline:**
> CloudTeam gives your distributed team in SG, PH, and MY one place to manage tasks, share async video updates, and run standups automatically. Your morning in Singapore catches up with your evening in Manila — no extra meetings needed.

**Social proof bar:**
> Join 8,000+ remote teams across Southeast Asia. Trusted by Ninja Van, Love Bonito, and more.

**Primary CTA button:**
> Start for Free

**Secondary CTA:**
> Watch a 2-minute demo

**Risk-reduction micro-copy (below CTA):**
> No credit card required. No long-term contract. Cancel anytime.

### A/B testing recommendation
Test swapping the subheadline to lead with the automated standup feature specifically: "Stop scheduling standup calls across 3 time zones. CloudTeam runs them automatically." This tests whether a single sharp pain point converts better than a feature overview.

## Variation C: Before-After-Bridge + Community Proof

**Framework rationale:** The Before-After-Bridge pattern (inspired by StoreHub's proven "From X to Y" structure) is inherently scannable and works across English proficiency levels. The short parallel structure grabs attention, while community proof and specific feature callouts build credibility. This variation leads with the transformation rather than the problem.

**Best suited for:** Ops leads and managers who already know their current workflow is broken and are looking for a clear upgrade path. The transformation framing helps them picture the outcome quickly.

### Hero copy

**Headline:**
> From Scattered to Synced

**Subheadline:**
> Your team works across Singapore, the Philippines, and Malaysia. CloudTeam brings everyone together with real-time task boards, async video updates, and automated standup reports — so no one starts the day guessing what happened overnight.

**Social proof bar:**
> 8,000+ teams across Southeast Asia run on CloudTeam. See why Ninja Van and Love Bonito made the switch.

**Primary CTA button:**
> Try CloudTeam Free

**Secondary CTA:**
> See how it works

**Risk-reduction micro-copy (below CTA):**
> Set up in 5 minutes. No credit card required. Free for small teams.

### A/B testing recommendation
Test the headline "From Scattered to Synced" against "From Time Zone Chaos to Team Clarity" to compare brevity vs. specificity. The shorter version is more scannable on mobile; the longer version names the exact pain.
