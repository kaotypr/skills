## Context

| Dimension | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Target market** | Philippines |
| **Product type** | SME tool (social commerce — order tracking, inventory, customer messaging) |
| **Target audience** | Millennial women (solo entrepreneurs, micro-businesses selling on Facebook and Instagram) |
| **Platform** | Email (onboarding welcome sequence) |
| **Objective** | Onboarding — activate new users, build trust, drive first workflow completion |

**Key stats for social proof:** 15,000+ merchants, 500K+ orders processed per month.

**Tone calibration:** Warm professional leaning toward friendly expert. Philippines audience allows for slightly warmer, more personal tone than pan-SEA copy. Facebook is the "default internet" here — reference it naturally.

## Variation A: JTBD + Friction Removal

**Framework rationale:** JTBD grounds each email in a specific job the merchant needs done. Friction Removal addresses the real anxieties of solo entrepreneurs who have limited time and may worry about setup complexity. This combination keeps copy action-oriented and removes barriers fast.

**Best suited for:** Philippines (primary), also works for Malaysia and pan-SEA with minor adjustments.

## A/B Testing Recommendations

1. **Subject lines:** Test Variation A (benefit-driven: "Your first sale on ShopFlow") against Variation C (curiosity-driven: "What 15,000 sellers know") for Email 3. Filipino audiences respond well to both, but curiosity hooks may drive higher open rates.

2. **Email 1 approach:** Test Variation A (feature-list welcome) against Variation B (Before-After contrast). Measure which drives higher shop-connection rates within 24 hours.

3. **Social proof placement:** Test placing the "15,000+ merchants" line at the top of Email 1 (Variation C) versus the bottom (Variation A). Community numbers at the top may build trust faster, but feature-first may be clearer for users who just signed up.

4. **P.S. lines:** Test "Reply to this email" (personal) against "Message us on Facebook" (platform-native for Filipino users) to see which drives more support engagement.

5. **CTA wording:** Test "Connect your shop now" against "Set up your shop — takes 3 minutes" to determine whether the speed promise lifts conversion.

## Self-Check: Anti-Pattern Verification

| Anti-pattern | Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Over-Americanized tone | Clear | Warm, inviting tone throughout. No aggressive commands. |
| Idiom dependency | Clear | No idioms used. All language is literal and concrete. |
| Grade 10+ readability | Clear | Short sentences, simple vocabulary, one idea per sentence. Estimated Grade 5-6. |
| Unsupported superlatives | Clear | No "leading" or "best" claims. All proof is quantified (15,000+ merchants, 500K+ orders). |
| Individual hero framing | Clear | Framed as joining a community of sellers, not individual greatness. |
| Translation artifacts | Clear | Written natively in English, not translated. |
| Missing social proof | Clear | Community numbers in every email. Peer stories in Email 3 across all variations. |
| Desktop-first copy | Clear | Short paragraphs, scannable structure, mobile-friendly formatting. |
| Faking local slang | Clear | Standard warm English throughout. One natural Taglish phrase in Variation A Email 3 ("sold out na pala") used in a quoted context to feel authentic without forcing slang in brand voice. |
| Linear funnel assumption | Clear | Each email works independently. P.S. lines and share CTAs support non-linear, social discovery. Facebook share CTA in Variation C supports peer-to-peer referral. |
